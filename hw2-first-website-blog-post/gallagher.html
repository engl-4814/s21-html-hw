How The Creative Journey Started

Summer of 2016 I found an old crochet bikini in a drawer at my grandmothers cabin in Norway. The bikini was a mustard yellow vintage style piece, so old that the elastic had long since rotted. Could I make something like this? I wondered. It was summer and I had plenty of time on my hands. Signe, my great aunt, was and still is a master crocheter and knitter. The next day I biked over to her house on the top of the hill. Signa is a warm lady, always happy for company. She welcomed me in with a strong hug and was delighted to hear of my mission. Her teaching style was strict and I learned quickly how to put the stitches together.

Eager to start, I immediately started on my first piece: A simple cotton bralette. I was hooked. I spent the next 6 months feverishly crochetting, learning with every project. By the spring time the next year I was crocheting every day in class to keep my hand occupied. My classmates took notice and interest in purchasing my pieces grew. That spring season I was blessed and sold almost 50 tops to girls in my highschool. I started fantasizing about growing and upgrading my business...
